How robindrop works

Full transparency: what is on-chain, what our bot does, and what you are trusting.

The launch

robindrop is a launch layer on the pons protocol. Your wallet signs launchToken() on the pons factory directly. We never touch your funds during launch: the launch fee and any dev buy go straight to the factory. Supply is fixed at 1,000,000,000, the Uniswap V3 pool is locked forever, graduation happens at 4.2 ETH of paired principal.

The fee vault

When you pick a managed fee mode (holder drops, team split, buyback and burn), robindrop generates a fresh wallet, the vault, and its address goes into feeWallet at launch. The locker then pays all creator fees to that vault.

Be clear about the trust model: the vault's private key is held by robindrop, encrypted with AES-256-GCM, with the encryption key living only in server environment variables. Your token, your pool and your own funds stay non-custodial. The creator fee stream for managed modes is custodial by design: that is what lets the bot pay hundreds of holders without you signing every transfer. If you do not want that, pick "Keep it" and the fees go to your own wallet, robindrop untouched.

Drop to holders, the trench mode

The bot claims fees from the locker via collectFees(), unwraps WETH, and pays rounds by your schedule:

  • Holder range: ranks #1 to #1000 by balance. The pool, the locker, the vault and the burn address never count. Your dev wallet is excluded too.
  • Rounds: 1 to 30. Each round distributes its % of whatever the vault holds when the round fires, split evenly across the range.
  • Triggers: market cap milestones (ladder x2, x3, or +$X steps from your starting cap) or a plain timer (every N hours).
  • After the schedule: the vault keeps flushing 100% of new fees at the same cadence, so late fees still reach holders.

Every payout is a plain transfer from the vault, visible on Blockscout, and listed on the token page.

Team split and burn

Team split: up to 20 wallets with percentages summing to 100. The bot forwards ETH and token fees by those shares whenever the vault holds enough to be worth the gas.

Buyback and burn: token-side fees go straight to 0x...dEaD. WETH-side fees are swapped into your token on your own pool and burned too.

What can go wrong

  • Holder snapshots come from the Blockscout API. If it lags, a round waits for the next bot cycle rather than paying the wrong list.
  • Rounds fire when the bot's market cap read crosses your target. Wicks can trigger a round; that is the same tradeoff trench made.
  • If a payout would give each holder dust, the round waits until the vault is worth splitting.
